Kinetic Awareness — Moving Art for Change

A 9-week program where students explore the physics of motion, culminating in an art event to raise environmental awareness and build interactive kinetic sculptures.

Duration

9 Weeks

Core Disciplines

Physics & Art

Final Project

Kinetic Sculpture

Phase 1: The Physics of Motion (4 Weeks)

In the initial phase, learners choose to explore the physics of motion through hands-on activities. They will learn how to use the scientific process as a tool to gain a better understanding of the connection between Mathematics and the principles of motion in Physics. This foundational knowledge is key to the creative work that follows.

  • Hands-on physics experiments.
  • Using the scientific process to ask and answer questions.
  • Connecting mathematical concepts to real-world movement.

Final Phase: Art for Environmental Awareness

In the culmination of the program, students get together and organize an event to raise awareness about an environmental concern. Each group of learners uses their understanding of physics to build an interactive prototype, demonstrating their concerns through a powerful blend of art, physics, and creativity.

The final challenge is to build a kinetic sculpture that represents a favorite concept in motion and conveys a powerful environmental message.
